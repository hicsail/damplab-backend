**PCR

Step 1: Master Mix Preparation
  1. Perform all procedures on ice – grab Styrofoam bin to fill with ice. Gather all needed materials from table below from -20C mini-freezer. Make sure to grab your diluted concentrations and not your original sample/primers.  ***Be careful to not warm the polymerase with your hands – always keep on ice!***
  2.  Label a new microcentrifuge tube ‘MM’ for your master mix. 
  3. Label PCR strip with your DNA template names on each tube (this is what will go into the thermocycler once finished) as well as ‘PCR’ on side of tube 
  4. Place your template DNA, primers, dNTPs, and buffer onto the 37C heat block to thaw.
  5. Prepare PCR reaction based on the table below, the polymerase our lab uses is NEB Q5 DNA polymerase 
  6. When prepping master mix, keep the following in mind:
  • Always pipette largest → smallest volumes (ie start with water first, and then proceed down the table). 
  • Make sure to vortex and spin down each reagent (except the polymerase!!) before adding to MM tube. 
  • When adding to MM tube, pipette at bottom of tube & thoroughly mix all contents together a few times for each new reagent.
  • Always add polymerase last once all the other reagents (except sample) have been added to the MM tube.
